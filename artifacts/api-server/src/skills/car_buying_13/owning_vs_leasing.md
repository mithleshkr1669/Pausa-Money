Making the decision: lease or buy
When it comes to getting a vehicle, you typically have two main options: leasing or buying. Each choice comes with its own set of advantages and disadvantages. In this article, we'll explore what leasing and buying entail, comparing their costs and benefits to help you make an informed decision.
What is leasing?
Leasing a car is similar to renting. Leasing is only available at dealerships, and only for new cars. You pay every month to use the car, but it’s not yours to keep. Leases typically last two to four years. At the end of the lease, you have the option to buy the car at a predetermined price, lease another car, or return the car to the dealership.
Pros of leasing
Lower monthly payments: Monthly payments for leasing are often less than buying because you're only paying for using the car temporarily.
Always have a new car: Leasing means you can drive a brand-new car every few years and not worry about selling it when you want to switch.
Fewer repair costs: Most leased cars are covered by a warranty, so many repair costs aren’t your problem.
Cons of leasing
Limits on driving: There's a cap on how many miles you can drive. If you go over, you’ll pay extra.
No ownership: You don't own the car, so all those payments don't end with you keeping the car.
Extra costs for damage: If the car gets too banged up beyond normal use, you might pay more at the end of the lease.
Due at signing: You often need to pay a significant amount upfront when you start a lease.
Always a payment: If you keep leasing cars, you'll always have a car payment to make.
Car buying
Buying a car means it ends up being entirely yours. You can pay for it all at once or get a loan. When you finish paying off the loan, there are no more car payments, and the car is all yours.
Pros of buying
Ownership: After the car is paid off, it's all yours. There are no more payments, and you can do whatever you want with the car.
No driving limits: You can drive as much as you want without worrying about extra charges for going over a certain mileage.
Customize freely: Since the car is yours, feel free to customize it however you see fit.
Cons of buying
Higher monthly payments: Usually, the monthly payments on a loan for buying a car are higher than leasing payments.
Value drops: Most cars lose value over time. When you go to sell, you probably won't get all your money back.
Repair costs: Once the warranty is up, any repairs are your financial responsibility.

Wrapping up
Leasing could be your go-to if you like driving a new car every few years and don't mind continuous payments. However, buying might be better for you if you want to eventually be free of monthly payments, love the idea of no driving restrictions, and want the freedom to customize your car.
Consider what matters most to you in your day-to-day life and future goals. Your decision to lease or buy your next car should align with what you value the most
