How to evaluate quality of college education: What are the indicators and sources?
When choosing a college, it's essential to consider factors like its reputation, how hard it is to get in, potential starting salaries, and graduation rates. By exploring various resources, you can gather the necessary information to make a well-informed decision when picking the right school for you.
Why is quality of education investment important?
When thinking about college, it's important to consider if it's worth it for what you want to study. This means thinking about if what you get out of it is worth the money, time, and effort you put in. People want different things, like making new friends, getting a better job, or just gaining new knowledge. How good the college experience is can be different for everyone and depends on the school, what you study, and what you're looking for.
How do you judge the quality of a college?
College is one of the biggest investments you can make, and, naturally, you want to make sure that after you put all the time and money in it, you receive back something that is of good quality. But, how do you judge the quality of a college? Here are some ways to do it.
Research academic reputation
Academic reputation is how well-known and respected a school is because of its programs, teachers, and successful students. It's important because if a school has a good reputation, it can open up a lot of doors for you, from getting a job, to promotions, and recognitions. Some famous schools like Harvard or Yale have great reputations because they have really good professors and students who graduate and do great things.
To find out about a school's academic reputation, you can look at school websites or check out websites that rank colleges, like U.S. News & World Report. But, keep in mind, just because a school is famous doesn't always mean it's perfect for you!
Investigate selectivity and rigor
Another important thing to consider is a school's selectivity and rigor. Selectivity is how hard it is to get into a school, and rigor is how tough the classes are. This matters because a school that's harder to get into and has challenging classes can mean you're learning from better professors and students who also work hard.
To find out about selectivity and rigor, you can look at the school's website to see their acceptance rates, test scores, and what kind of classes they offer. This will help you find a college that has the right level of challenge for you.
Find out the average starting salary
Another thing to think about is how much money people usually make when they start working after going to a specific college. This helps you see if the stuff you learn at that college is valuable and in-demand. For example, an engineering graduate might make more money when they start working than someone who studied philosophy.
To find this information, you can look online or ask the college you're interested in to help you. Knowing how much money you could make after finishing college can help you decide if a certain college or field of study is right for you.
Remember to look at graduation rates
When looking for a good college, you should also think about graduation rates. Graduation rates show how many students actually finish their degrees at a college. It's important because it can help you understand if people usually finish their studies or have trouble at that school. For example, a school with a high graduation rate means that lots of students finish their degrees, and it could be a good sign.
To find graduation rates, you can visit websites like the National Center for Education Statistics, or just check the college's website. Knowing about graduation rates can help you pick a college that helps students succeed.
Is there an easier way to research a college?
Yes. Sometimes, going to each individual college website and researching all the things mentioned above can be exhausting. But, there are websites like CollegeData.com where all this information is complied into a single source that is easy to search.
Another great source of information are current students and of the college. They can give you real-life examples of what it's like to be a student there. They can tell you about clubs, classes, or even the food in the cafeteria.
To find current students or alumni, you can try going on a college tour, joining social media groups, or asking people you know if they have friends or family who go there. By talking to them, you'll get a better idea of what it's really like at that college.
How to evaluate the quality of education investment for non-college choices
Are you looking for ways to learn new skills without going to college? This article will help you figure out if non-college education options, like online courses or certification programs, are worth your time and money.
Evaluating the quality of education investment for non-college options
Sometimes, college might not be the best choice for everyone. There are many other ways to learn and gain valuable skills without getting a college degree. These can include vocational training, online courses, certification programs, or self-directed learning. But how can you tell if these options are worth your time and money? Let's take a look at some of the things you may want to think about.
How do you judge the quality of a school or a program?
Paying for training, certification, or online education can sometimes cost just as much as going to college. Because of that, it is important to make sure that what you are paying for and hoping to get is of good quality. Here is what to consider:
Check for accreditation
Accreditation means that the program has been checked by experts and meets high-quality standards. Having completed an accredited program can help you find better jobs and make sure you're learning valuable skills.
For example, a certified automotive technician program that is accredited by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) would be a good choice. On the other hand, a non-accredited course might not be very helpful or respected by employers.
To find out if a program is accredited, you can look for information on their website or contact the accrediting organization for your chosen field. The U.S. Department of Education has a list of organizations that check if the education provided is accredited, too.

AACSB accreditation, awarded by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business, means that this business school meets high-quality standards. Image credit: public domain
Look for clear learning objectives and outcomes
Learning objectives are the goals of the program, and outcomes are the skills you'll have when you finish. For example, if you're taking a coding course, a learning objective might be to understand how to write code in a specific language, and the outcome would be your ability to create a small app or website.
To find out the learning objectives and outcomes, check the course or program description on their website, or ask the instructors for more details. Knowing what you'll learn and how it can help you ensures you're choosing a non-college option that's worth your time and effort.
Consider the return and payoff
In other words: think about what you are getting in return and how much it means to you. Think about things like how much money you could make, the jobs you could get, and how happy it might make you. Then, think about the costs of the program. To figure out if it's worth it, look at things like the type of job, the industry, where you live, your experience, and how much money you might make.
Look at completion and certification rates
Lastly, when picking a non-college education option, it's important to know how likely it is for students to finish the program and get a certificate. This is called the completion and certification rate. The higher the rate, the more likely you'll be successful in the program, too.
For example, let's say you're choosing between two online coding courses: one has a

completion rate. The first option is probably better because more students finish and get their certificate.
You can usually find these rates on the program's website or by asking someone who works there. Remember, a good program should have high completion and certification rates to show that it's helping students reach their goals.
For-profit programs and schools
Some non-college education options might only care about making money and not really teaching you what you need to know. These programs can take your money but not help you get a good job or learn useful skills. One example is the ITT Technical Institute, which took students' money but didn't provide a good education.

So when you're looking at non-college options, make sure to do your research and watch out for programs that seem too good to be true. Always check for things like accreditation, learning objectives, and completion rates before you decide to join a program. It's essential to find a program that truly cares about your education and future success.
